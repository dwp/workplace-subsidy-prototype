Using workplace health to help an employee 

An employer may have concerns about an employee because:

  • there's been a change in how they're performing at work
  • they've been absent more often than normal 
  • they're long-term sick (off work sick for more than 4 weeks)

If you have concerns, you can ask your employee if they'll agree to a workplace health assessment. 

They do not have to agree to it, but it's a good idea as it can help them:

  • return to work 
  • get any support they might need

What happens in a workplace health assessment 

During the assessment, a workplace health professional will talk to your employee. They could be a doctor, a nurse, or other health provider. 

They will ask your employee about:

  • their health and any treatment they're having
  • their usual work activities
  • their general lifestyle and activities outside work 

The conversation is confidential. The workplace health professional will not share anything with you without your employee's consent.  

A workplace health assessment is not meant to find out what an employee's health condition or disability is. It's a discussion about a known health concern.

What happens after the assessment

The workplace health professional will prepare a report. They'll check with your employee if they can show it to you. Your employee can also choose whether they want to see the report first, or at the same time as you. 

The report will contain advice and recommendations, which could include:

  • adjustments to their workspace, such as a bigger screen or ergonomic chair
  • a phased return to work or different working hours
  • a referral to other services, like physiotherapy or counselling

You should discuss the recommendations with your employee and decide the best course of action.
