What health assessments mean as part of this scheme

Workplace health assessments, also known as occupational health assessments, can include fitness for work, return to work assessments or management referrals.

An assessment focuses on an employee’s work environment. It looks at how their job might affect their health or how their health may impact their work.

An assessment could include a discussion about an employee’s:

  • health and work activities
  • return to work plans
  • workplace adjustments
  • onward support

Services that are not included in this scheme

Occupational health (OH) providers cannot carry out the following services as part of this scheme:

  • health surveillance
  • health promotion
  • health risk assessments
  • advice on organisational policy to help with legal compliance
  • pre-employment or post-offer health assessments
  • clinical interventions to manage health risk, for example vaccinations
  • knowledge management support, such as sickness absence record keeping and data analysis
  • clinical rehabilitation or treatment services
  • training, instruction or capacity building for managers or leaders
  • case management
  • providing medical records for civil prosecutions

Benefits of joining the scheme

OH providers can:

  • improve SME access to specialist workplace health assessments
  • establish relationships with new clients
  • get potential repeat business
